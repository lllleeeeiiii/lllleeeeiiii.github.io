Sauteed Veggies

Heres a simple and quick recipe for making sauteed veggies thats perfect for a busy college student!


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (fresh or frozen; examples: bell peppers, broccoli, zucchini, carrots, or any veggies you like)
  • 1 tablespoon of oil(olive oil, vegetable oil, or any cooking oil you prefer)
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic (minced) or 1 teaspoon of garlic powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper(to taste)
  • Optional seasonings:soy sauce, Italian seasoning, or any spices you enjoy (e.g., paprika, chili flakes)

Materials:

  • Large skillet or frying pan
  • Wooden spoon or spatula
  • Cutting board
  • Knife
  • Serving bowl

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Veggies:
      -Wash and chop: If using fresh vegetables, wash them thoroughly. Cut them into bite-sized pieces using a cutting board and knife. If youâ€™re using frozen veggies, you can skip this step!

  2. Heat the Pan:
      - Add oil: Place a large skillet on the stove over medium heat. Add 1 tablespoon of oil to the pan and let it heat up for a minute.

  3. Season:
      - Add seasoning: Season the veggies with salt, pepper, and any other optional seasonings you like (like soy sauce or Italian seasoning). Stir to combine and cook for another minute to let the flavors blend.

  4. Serve:
      -Transfer to a bowl: Once the veggies are cooked and seasoned, transfer them to a serving bowl. If you want to add any cooked protein, mix it in at this stage.

  5. Enjoy!
      -Dig in: Enjoy your delicious and healthy sauteed veggies! They make a great side dish or a base for a main meal.

  6. Quick Tips:
      -Customizable: You can use any vegetables you have on hand. This is a great way to use up leftovers or whatever you find in your fridge!
      -Add flavor: Experiment with different spices and herbs to find what you like best.
      -You can pre-chop vegetables on the weekend and store them in the fridge for quick meals during the week.


Enjoy your quick and healthy sauteed veggies!
